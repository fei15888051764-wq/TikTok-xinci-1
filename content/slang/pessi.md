**"Pessi"** is what Cristiano Ronaldo fans call Lionel Messi when they want to remind everyone that half his goals came from twelve yards out.

It's a portmanteau of "penalty" and "Messi" — a salty nickname born from football rivalry that stuck around long enough to become its own meme. The word itself is an insult wrapped in wordplay, and the image macro that came with it is even worse.

## Usage & Context

**"Pessi" is a derogatory nickname for Lionel Messi used primarily by rival fans — especially Cristiano Ronaldo supporters — to mock him for seemingly scoring most of his goals through penalty kicks rather than open-play skill.**

The term shows up in football debates on X, Instagram comments, TikTok football edits, and meme pages whenever Messi converts a penalty. It's not just about the goals — it's about diminishing his overall legacy by implying he's dependent on the easiest scoring opportunity in the sport.

The nickname also spawned a viral image macro: an altered photo showing Messi as elderly, bald, and toothless, which started circulating in 2022 and became a staple in TikTok football skits by early 2023. The crude, deliberately ugly edit adds a visual layer to the insult — it's not just calling him a penalty merchant, it's roasting his entire appearance.

> **X post:** "Pessi scores another pen and his fans act like he just dribbled past 11 players 💀"

> **TikTok comment:** "They don't call him Pessi for nothing, 3 goals today and all 3 from the spot"

> **Group chat:** "here we go again, pessi time 😂 another penalty for argentina"

> **Instagram reply:** "Penaldo vs Pessi debate is the only thing keeping football Twitter alive"

## Origin Story

The nickname was coined on August 23rd, 2012, during a live thread on 4chan's /sp/ (sports) board while fans were watching the Supercopa de España match between FC Barcelona and Real Madrid. As the match progressed and Messi took a penalty, frustrated Ronaldo fans in the thread started calling him "Pessi" — and the nickname stuck.

Throughout 2013, "Pessi" continued to appear in /sp/ threads, gradually spreading to other football forums and social media. On October 20th, 2020, the verified Twitter account @DoentesPFutebol posted a photo of Messi preparing a penalty kick with the simple caption "Pessi," pulling roughly 1,700 likes in three years. On December 22nd, 2020, the term was officially added to Urban Dictionary by user Not going to tell, cementing its place in online football slang.

The altered image macro — showing a decrepit, elderly version of Messi — first surfaced in 2022 and went viral on TikTok in early 2023, often used in crude video skits where creators would lip-sync to football commentary while the distorted image flashed on screen.

## Cultural Significance

"Pessi" is part of a broader internet tradition of creating derogatory portmanteaus for famous athletes. Messi fans responded in kind by calling Ronaldo "Penaldo" — the exact same formula applied to their rival. The two nicknames now function as shorthand in football debates, with fans of each player wielding them like weapons.

The meme's longevity is remarkable for internet standards. Born in 2012, it's still actively used in 2026 — fourteen years later. That staying power comes from the endless nature of the Messi vs. Ronaldo debate, which shows no signs of ending even as both players enter the final stages of their careers.

GEBILAOWANG's take: the funniest thing about "Pessi" isn't the insult itself — it's that Messi fans got so mad about it they literally invented "Penaldo" as a comeback, and now both sides are just yelling the same joke at each other. Football tribalism really does make you creative.
