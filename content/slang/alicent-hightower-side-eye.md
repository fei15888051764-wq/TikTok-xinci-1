**"Alicent Hightower Side Eye"** is one of those reaction images that says "wait, run that back" without a single word of dialogue.

It's a warped screenshot of House of the Dragon's Alicent glancing sideways in pure disbelief, and it became the fandom's favorite way to react to a take so confusing it short-circuits your brain.

## Usage & Context

**"Alicent Hightower Side Eye" is a reaction image of House of the Dragon's Alicent Hightower glancing sideways in disbelief, used to express confusion, disbelief, or an uncomfortable realization.**

Drop it under any post that makes you do a double-take — a wild take, a suspicious text, a plot twist you didn't see coming. Fans also use the distorted face to tease Alicent's storyline, joking that the once-scheming queen has gone weirdly passive compared to her book counterpart.

> **DM:** "he said he's 'not like other guys' 💀 *sends Alicent side eye*"

> **Friend chat:** "my roommate ate my leftovers and blamed the dog — Alicent side eye"

## Cultural Significance

It became one of the most-shared HOTD season three jokes, but not without pushback — on July 8th, 2026, Redditors in /r/HOTDGreens argued the warped edits were disrespectful to actress Olivia Cooke, who'd previously deactivated Instagram over hate. Others countered that the jokes target the character and the show's direction, not the performer.

## Origin Story

The face comes from House of the Dragon season three, episode three, "Rhaenyra Triumphant," which premiered July 5th, 2026. Brought in to identify a captured "prince," Alicent glances sideways with a baffled look before revealing the young man is an imposter she's never met. Screenshots hit Reddit's Freefolk the next day, then spread to X, where users kept warping the frame into increasingly absurd edits.
