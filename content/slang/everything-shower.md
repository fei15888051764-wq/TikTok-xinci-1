An everything shower is the opposite of your daily five-minute rinse. It's the weekly (or monthly, no judgment) event where you do every single grooming task in one session: hair masks, exfoliation, shaving, deep cleansing, the full catalog. You go in a regular person and come out a lightly steamed, fully reset version of yourself.

TikTok treats it like a religious ceremony, and honestly, the devotion makes sense once you've had one.

## What actually happens in there

The ritual runs in three phases. Pre-shower: playlist on, candle lit, maybe dry brushing or a hair oil treatment while you mentally prepare. The shower itself: clarifying scalp treatment, double shampoo, conditioner, body scrub, shaving, double cleanse, in whatever order your personal scripture demands. Post-shower: moisturizer on damp skin, body oil, robe, collapse.

Total time ranges from a disciplined 30 minutes to a genuinely concerning four hours. When E! covered the trend in February 2023, the tag #showertok had already cleared 6 billion views, and one creator's post-shower review summed up the appeal: legs shaking, skin glowing, "I am now flawless."

## The expert take

Dermatologists are on board with one asterisk. Dr. Rachel Ho's warning to the trend was simple: more steps means more chances to irritate your skin, and stacking strong actives in one session can wreck your barrier faster than it glows you up. Translation: the everything shower works, but your skin doesn't need everything, everything, every time.

There's also the water question, which comes up in every comment section. A two-hour shower is a lot of water, and even the biggest fans tend to frame this as a weekly ritual rather than a daily habit. Once a week, cap the shower itself at a sane length, do the masks with the water off. Problem solved.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What does everything shower mean on TikTok?

It's a long, deluxe shower session where you complete your entire grooming routine in one go, from scalp treatments to body scrubs to shaving, usually as a weekly self-care reset.

### How long does an everything shower take?

Anywhere from 30 minutes to a few hours, depending on how many steps you include. The point is thoroughness, not speed, though dermatologists recommend keeping the actual water-on time reasonable.

### How often should you take an everything shower?

Most people treat it as a weekly or occasional ritual, not a daily habit. Daily is too much for your skin barrier and your water bill.
