A sneaky link is the person you see but never post. No stories together, no soft launch, no hard launch, nothing. Your friends might suspect something is going on because you keep "having errands" at 11pm, but there's no proof anywhere, and that's the whole point.

The term covers the person and sometimes the meet-up itself. You can have a sneaky link, and you can also say you're "about to go link" with the understanding that nobody's finding out.

## How a song made it a thing

Secret hookups obviously existed before 2021. They just didn't have a catchy name yet.

Then HXLLYWOOD dropped "Sneaky Link" and the chorus did what TikTok choruses do: "Girl, I can be your sneaky link (sneaky link) / Be my sneaky link (sneaky link)." By May 2021 the sound was everywhere, with Capital FM already running explainers for confused millennials on May 7th of that year. People started posting videos teasing their own situations, jacket half on, sneaking out the door, and the term locked in permanently.

Here's the part I find funny. The song is openly romantic about the arrangement. The internet took it and made it transactional. A term born from a smooth R&B hook now mostly gets used when someone wants to gossip about their coworker's suspicious Friday nights.

The concept kept evolving too. Netflix turned the same idea into a dating show format in 2025, which tells you the word fully crossed over from slang to standard vocabulary. Your aunt might not say it, but she'd understand it.

## Using it without incriminating yourself

A few things worth knowing before you start calling people sneaky links.

First, it's casual. Calling someone your sneaky link is almost the opposite of calling them your partner. It signals the situation is low-key. Probably not serious. Definitely not public. If someone you actually like hears you describe them that way, expect a conversation you don't want to have.

Second, the secrecy is the point, not a side effect. A relationship that's private because you're private people isn't a sneaky link situation. The term implies active hiding. Deleted tags, separate arrivals, "we're just friends" energy.

Third, it skews young. Teens and people in their early twenties say it constantly. Beyond that it starts sounding like you're trying too hard, same as most dating slang. Use it about others, sparingly, and it lands fine. Build your whole vocabulary around it and you're the person who says "no cap" in job interviews.

You'll mostly see it in three places: storytime videos ("so my sneaky link's girlfriend found my earring..."), comment sections investigating who's dating who, and group chats. So many group chats.

The group chat part deserves its own paragraph, honestly. Every friend group has that one person who swears they're single while their location keeps showing up across town at midnight. The term gave everyone a shorthand for the investigation. No more "who's that in your story?" Just: sneaky link? Case opened. Half the fun of the word is that it turned privacy into a team sport, where your friends treat your love life like a cold case they're determined to crack.

> "he said he's not dating anyone but bro got dropped off by the same car three Fridays in a row. sneaky link confirmed"

> @caitlinesss: not me waving at my sneaky link like we're coworkers 💀 we are not coworkers

Someone posts a photo dump with a suspiciously cropped shoulder in the corner of one picture. Top comment: "we see the sneaky link in slide 4 bestie."

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What does sneaky link mean on TikTok?

It refers to a person you secretly hook up with or meet up with, hidden from friends and social media. On TikTok it shows up in storytimes, dating content, and comment-section detective work about who's secretly seeing who.

### Is a sneaky link the same as a situationship?

Not quite. A situationship is an undefined relationship that both people are in openly. A sneaky link is defined by the hiding itself. Plenty of situationships are public, and a sneaky link usually knows exactly what it is.

### Where did sneaky link come from?

It went viral in May 2021 thanks to the song "Sneaky Link" by HXLLYWOOD, which blew up on TikTok and had media outlets publishing explainers within days.

### Is calling someone your sneaky link an insult?

Depends on delivery. Between friends joking around, it's harmless. Said about someone directly to them, it usually translates to "you're a secret I'm keeping," which most people don't love hearing.
