## Usage & Context

**"Spill the tea"** is the internet's official request for gossip. When someone says "spill the tea," they're not asking for beverage recommendations — they want the drama, the details, the whole messy story. And they want it now.

The phrase works in several variations. "What's the tea?" asks for the current gossip. "That's the tea" confirms the information as truth. "Sips tea" describes someone watching drama unfold from a safe distance. Each variation keeps the beverage metaphor alive while delivering maximum drama.

> **TikTok Comment:** "POV: your friend says 'I have news' and you immediately reply 'SPILL THE TEA' in all caps."

> **Group Chat:** "Friend: something happened at the party last night / Everyone: SPILL THE TEA / Friend: *typing...* / Everyone: THE SUSPENSE IS KILLING US / Friend: okay so basically..."

> **Twitter/X Post:** "When your coworker says 'did you hear about what happened in the meeting' and you have to pretend you weren't already told by three different people. 'Oh? Spill the tea.'"

The phrase has also spawned visual memes. The "sips tea" Kermit meme — where Kermit the Frog calmly drinks tea while chaos unfolds around him — became one of the most shared reaction images of the late 2010s. The combination of the phrase and the meme cemented "tea" as the universal symbol for gossip.

## Cultural Significance

"Spill the tea" represents one of the most successful crossovers from drag culture into mainstream internet language. The phrase originated in Black drag communities in the 1990s, where "tea" was coded language for truth and gossip. From there, it traveled through RuPaul's Drag Race, Twitter, and finally TikTok — picking up millions of users along the way.

The phrase's endurance is remarkable. Most internet slang has a lifespan of 2-3 years before becoming "cheugy." "Spill the tea" has been going strong for over a decade, which suggests it filled a genuine linguistic gap. English didn't have a fun, casual way to request gossip — until drag culture provided one.

The metaphor itself is perfect. Tea is hot. Tea gets spilled. And when tea spills, everyone gathers around to see the mess. The imagery is so apt that the phrase feels less like slang and more like common sense.

## Origin Story

"Spill the tea" originated in Black drag culture in the 1990s. In drag balls and clubs, "tea" was coded slang for "truth" — particularly the kind of truth that involved gossip, drama, and behind-the-scenes information. "Spilling the tea" meant sharing that truth, usually with theatrical flair.

The phrase gained mainstream exposure through RuPaul's Drag Race, which premiered in 2009. Contestants regularly asked each other to "spill the tea," introducing the phrase to millions of viewers who had never been to a drag show. By the mid-2010s, "spill the tea" had fully crossed over into general internet culture.

The "Kermit sips tea" meme, which emerged around 2014, gave the phrase visual form. The image of Kermit calmly drinking tea while drama unfolded became the perfect reaction image for observing gossip from a safe distance. The meme and the phrase reinforced each other, creating a self-sustaining cultural phenomenon.

> **TikTok Trend:** The #spillthetea hashtag has over 3.8 billion views as of July 2026. The most popular format features creators sharing celebrity gossip or personal drama while literally drinking tea, often with dramatic lighting and suspenseful music.
