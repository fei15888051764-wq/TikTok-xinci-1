Your Roman Empire is whatever your brain refuses to let go of. That thing you said in 2017. The Roman Empire itself, if you're a certain kind of guy. The phrase took over TikTok in 2023 as the answer to one question: "How often do you think about the Roman Empire?" And the honest answer, for a lot of men, was "daily."

The setup was always the same. A woman would ask her boyfriend or dad the question on camera, expecting "never," and instead get a thoughtful speech about aqueducts. The gap between expectation and answer was the whole joke. Then the phrase escaped its own meme. By late 2023, "my Roman Empire" had stopped meaning Rome. It meant anything that lives in your head permanently.

## My Roman Empire in the wild

The formula is simple: name the thing you can't quit.

> "the dress my ex wore to graduation is my roman empire"

> @someone: people ask about my hobbies. sir, the 2008 financial crisis is my roman empire

You'll also see it as a confession: "i think about the zodiac killer at least twice a week. my roman empire." The weirder the obsession, the better the post. Nobody's Roman Empire is normal. That's the point.

The original spark is documented: in August 2023, a Roman reenactor named Gaius Flavius posted an Instagram reel suggesting women ask the men in their lives this exact question and film the answers. Within weeks the trend had hundreds of millions of views, and mentions of the Roman Empire jumped 750% between August and September 2023. Brandwatch later put total TikTok views for the trend at 1.8 billion. One reel. That's all it took.

## How a joke question became an idiom

Most trends die in a month. This one mutated instead. The phrase survived because it filled a vocabulary gap: English had no casual word for "thing I think about constantly for no reason." "Obsession" sounds clinical. "Living rent free in my head" was already tired. "My Roman Empire" walked in and took the job.

You'll notice the gender angle faded fast. The first month was all "why do men love Rome so much," with historians getting dragged in to explain legions to TikTok. Then everyone quietly agreed the phrase was bigger than that. Now it's just a confession booth. You post your Roman Empire, the comments post theirs, and nobody has to explain why they still think about a sandwich they ate in 2019.

My take: it's one of the rare memes that got more useful after the joke died. People love a sanctioned way to admit they're weird. This phrase hands it to them with a smile. It also helps that saying "my Roman Empire" sounds faintly intellectual, even when the obsession is a reality TV fight from four years ago.
