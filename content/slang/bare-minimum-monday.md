Bare minimum Monday is exactly what it sounds like: on Monday, you do the bare minimum. Not because you're lazy, but because sprinting out of the weekend straight into peak productivity is how people burn out by Wednesday. You handle the essential two or three tasks, skip the heroic extras, and save your actual energy for Tuesday through Friday.

The term was coined by TikTok creator Marisa Jo Mayes in 2022, as her personal fix for the Sunday scaries. She described structuring Mondays around only what's strictly necessary, and the video resonated with millions of people who had apparently been doing the same thing in secret and feeling guilty about it. Now it had a name, and names legitimize.

## How it's different from quiet quitting

People constantly mix these two up, so let's be precise. Quiet quitting is passive: you're checked out of a job you resent, doing the minimum forever as a quiet protest. Bare minimum Monday is proactive: a one-day pacing strategy you choose for your own wellbeing, with the explicit goal of being more productive the rest of the week. One is disengagement. The other is energy management.

A typical bare minimum Monday looks like this: no meetings before 10, a short list of must-do tasks only, a real lunch break, maybe a walk, and zero guilt about any of it. The keyword is intentional. You're not slacking, you're tapering, the way athletes ease into a race week.

## The case for it, and the eye-roll

The defenders point to brutal numbers: surveys through 2025 kept finding huge chunks of the global workforce in chronic burnout, and Monday anxiety is basically a shared human condition at this point. Starting the week at 60% speed so you can finish it at 100% isn't weakness, it's just math.

The critics, mostly managers and think-piece writers, call it a rebrand of laziness and worry about what happens when Tuesday also starts looking negotiable. Both sides have a point, honestly. The strategy works beautifully for people with flexible or autonomous jobs and is basically unavailable to anyone whose Monday is scheduled by someone else.

My read on this: the trend was never really about Monday. It's workers admitting out loud that the always-on week was a bad design, and Monday was just the first wall they had permission to soften.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What does bare minimum monday mean on TikTok?

It's a work strategy, popularized by creator Marisa Jo Mayes in 2022, where you deliberately do only essential tasks on Mondays to ease into the week, reduce anxiety, and avoid burnout.

### Is bare minimum monday the same as quiet quitting?

No. Quiet quitting is permanently disengaging from a job you've given up on. Bare minimum monday is a one-day pacing strategy designed to make you more productive the rest of the week.

### Does bare minimum monday actually work?

Fans say starting slow kills Monday dread and boosts the rest of the week. It works best in flexible jobs; if your Monday schedule isn't yours to control, the strategy is harder to use.
