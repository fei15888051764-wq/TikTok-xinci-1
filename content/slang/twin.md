## Pronunciation
/twɪn/

## Multi-angle Interpretation

"Twin" on TikTok goes beyond literal siblings. It's used to describe:
- Someone who looks strikingly similar to you
- Someone who shares your exact vibe or energy
- Someone who agrees with you completely
- Someone who finishes your sentences

The term suggests a connection deeper than friendship—a recognition of sameness.

GEBILAOWANG's take: "twin" reflects Gen Z's desire for deep connection in a world that often feels isolating. Calling someone your "twin" is the highest form of social approval—it means "you are fundamentally like me, and that makes me feel seen."

## Origin & Usage

The slang usage of "twin" has been present in AAVE for decades but exploded on TikTok as users began posting "twin challenges" where they found their doppelgangers. The term expanded from looks to vibes to full identity alignment.

**Applicable crowds:** Everyone

**Usage taboos:** Don't use it to mock someone who looks like you in an unflattering way. "Twin" is always positive.
