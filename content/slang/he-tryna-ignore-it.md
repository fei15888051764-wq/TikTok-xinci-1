## Pronunciation
/hiː ˈtraɪnə ɪɡˈnɔːr ɪt/

## Multi-angle Interpretation

"He tryna ignore it" is a masterclass in saying everything while saying very little. The phrase captures the universal experience of watching someone pretend not to care while being visibly affected. The AAVE-influenced grammar ("tryna" for "trying to") adds authenticity and cultural specificity that standard English couldn't achieve.

The phrase works across genders despite the "he" pronoun—users regularly apply it to people of any gender. The "he" has become almost generic, functioning as a placeholder rather than a literal gender marker. This linguistic flexibility is common in internet slang, where phrases often transcend their original specificity.

GEBILAOWANG's take: this phrase is a perfect example of how TikTok audio culture creates linguistic memes. The original sound clip provided the delivery, tone, and context; users then extracted the phrase and applied it to infinite new situations. The result is a piece of language that carries the energy of its origin while being endlessly adaptable.

## Origin & Usage

"He tryna ignore it" originated from a viral TikTok sound clip in early 2026. The original video featured someone narrating another person's obvious attempt to pretend indifference, and the phrase's rhythmic delivery made it instantly memeable. Within weeks, it had been used in hundreds of thousands of videos.

The phrase's adaptability drove its longevity. It works as a caption, a comment, a text overlay, and even spoken dialogue. Its grammatical flexibility—users regularly modify it to "she tryna ignore it," "they tryna ignore it," etc.—has helped it maintain relevance beyond the original audio's peak.

**Applicable crowds:** Meme enthusiasts, relationship content creators, anyone who appreciates calling out performative behavior

**Usage taboos:** Using it to mock genuine emotional processing or trauma responses is inappropriate. The phrase is meant to call out obvious, performative indifference—not to shame people for taking time to process difficult feelings.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I use this for women too?**
A: Absolutely. Despite the "he," the phrase is gender-neutral in practice. "She tryna ignore it" and "they tryna ignore it" are both common variations.

**Q: Is this AAVE?**
A: Yes, the grammatical structure and pronunciation reflect AAVE influences. As with all AAVE-derived slang, use it respectfully and understand its cultural origins.

**Q: What's the original sound?**
A: The original viral sound featured the phrase delivered in a specific rhythmic pattern that emphasized the "tryna" and the implied eye-roll in "ignore it." While the original creator's video has been lost in the algorithmic shuffle, the phrase has taken on a life of its own.
