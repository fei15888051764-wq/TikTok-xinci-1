## How to Use It

The Great Meme Reset is the internet's collective New Year's resolution: delete brainrot, bring back 2016 memes. No cap, that's literally the whole pitch.

You drop it whenever someone complains that memes aren't funny anymore. Or when your fyp suddenly looks like a 2016 time capsule and you need an explanation.

> **场景:** "Why is my entire fyp just Rage Comics and Doge rn?" "Great Meme Reset, bestie. We're so back."

> **场景:** "I refuse to learn one more brainrot word." "Same. I'm fully Great Meme Reset-pilled."

> **场景:** "Did bro just post a Harambe tribute in 2026?" "He's participating in the Reset. Show some respect."

> **场景:** "These new memes last like 3 days max." "That's why people wanted a Great Meme Reset in the first place."

It works as a noun, a hashtag, and occasionally a personality trait (chronically online behavior, but make it nostalgic).

## Where It Started

The origin story is honestly pretty well documented for once.

On March 13, 2025, TikToker @joebro909 posted a comedy skit about the so-called "meme drought" — that painfully dry stretch when nothing funny was going viral. The text overlay read "The great meme reset," and the skit's solution was unhinged: erase every meme from history and start over. The video pulled over 254K views and 34K likes, according to the Daily Dot.

The idea kept snowballing. In April 2025, Redditor yukifujita posted an Advice Dog meme captioned "We need a reset" and farmed roughly 18,000 upvotes. Then on September 16, TikToker @ieat_mangoes declared current memes "so buns" and formally proposed the Great Meme Reset, pulling another 13K+ likes.

By late 2025 it was a full campaign. TikToker @golden._vr posted a countdown-style video — "The last resort for memes... December 31st, 2025, 11:59. Memes are rising from the grave" — which racked up around 370,000 likes. Wired covered the movement on November 19, 2025, and creator Noah Glenn Carter told them the plan plainly: on January 1, 2026, everyone resets all memes and goes back to the originals.

So did it happen? Kind of. January 2026 feeds genuinely filled up with Big Chungus, classic Doge, Ugandan Knuckles, and deep-fried formats. By July 2026, the Reset is still being name-dropped in viral moment roundups — less an organized event now, more a permanent vibe.

## Why It's Viral

Three reasons, fr fr.

First, meme fatigue is real. When memes started living and dying within 72 hours, people got exhausted. The Reset promised a return to memes that "had a story behind them," as Carter put it — or at least made sense.

Second, it's anti-AI slop. Know Your Meme editor-in-chief Don Caldwell told Wired that brainrot memes are "low-effort and nonsensical," and people crave memes with actual substance. The timing matched perfectly with Jack Dorsey backing diVine, a Vine revival that literally blocks AI-generated content.

Third, the irony. College of Charleston professor Ryan Milner asked the question everyone's thinking: is this a genuine movement, or just a meme about memes? That debate alone keeps the phrase rent free in comment sections.

## GEBILAOWANG's take

Real talk? The Great Meme Reset was never going to work as an organized campaign — you can't reset culture by committee, and Gen Alpha did not care about your Doge calendar. But as a vibe shift, it lowkey won. The anti-brainrot sentiment it normalized is everywhere now, and every "2026 is the new 2016" post is basically the Reset wearing a fake mustache. The movement "failed" and still changed what people post. That's the most internet outcome possible.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What does the Great Meme Reset mean on TikTok?

It's a movement that called for all memes to revert to their 2010s "golden era" starting January 1, 2026. Out with brainrot and AI slop, in with Big Chungus and Rage Comics.

### Did the Great Meme Reset actually happen?

Yes and no. January 2026 genuinely saw floods of retro memes, but brainrot obviously survived. Most people now treat it as a successful vibe shift rather than a literal reset.

### Who started the Great Meme Reset?

TikToker @joebro909 posted the first "great meme reset" skit in March 2025. Creators like @ieat_mangoes, @golden._vr, and Noah Glenn Carter turned it into an actual campaign later that year.

### Why 2016 specifically?

2016 is widely remembered as peak meme culture — Harambe, Doge's prime, dank memes with actual formats. It also became shorthand for "before the algorithm ruined everything."

### Is the Great Meme Reset still a thing in 2026?

As a scheduled event, it's over. As a phrase, it's stuck around — people use it whenever retro formats trend or whenever brainrot discourse flares up again.
