## What Does 'Monkey-Barring' Mean? The Toxic Dating Trend Explained (2026)

By **GEBILAOWANG** | Published: July 6, 2026

"Monkey-barring" is a 2025-2026 dating slang term describing the toxic behavior of maintaining a current relationship while actively seeking the next one — only letting go of the old partner after securing a new one. Named after the playground equipment, the term went viral on TikTok and has been covered by Cosmopolitan, USA TODAY, and relationship experts.

**The Psychology Behind Monkey-Barring and Why It's So Common**

The term draws its metaphor from monkey bars — the playground equipment where children swing from bar to bar, never letting go of one until they've secured the next. In dating, this manifests as someone who won't break up with their current partner until they've lined up a replacement. It's distinct from casual dating or exploring options; monkey-barring implies active deception and emotional dishonesty.

The behavior often stems from fear of being alone or low self-esteem — the person needs constant validation from a romantic partner and can't tolerate even brief periods of singleness. On TikTok, #monkeybarring videos typically feature storytimes about discovering a partner was already talking to their "next" while still in the relationship. The shared experiences have created a sense of solidarity among those who've been victim to the behavior. GEBILAOWANG's take: monkey-barring is particularly insidious because it denies both partners the opportunity for an honest breakup. The person being monkey-barred doesn't get closure — they find out they've been a placeholder. And the new partner enters a relationship built on the foundation of deception. It's lose-lose, yet remarkably common in the era of dating apps where alternatives are always just a swipe away.

**Real Usage in Native Context**

> *Comment:* "She had her next man lined up before she even broke up with me. I got monkey-barred so hard"

> *Advice video:* "If they're not willing to be single between relationships, they're a monkey-barrer. Red flag."

**FAQ**

- **Q:** What older expression is this most similar to? How is it different?
  **A:** "Monkey-barring" is similar to "overlapping relationships" or "lining up the next one," but the playground metaphor makes it more vivid and culturally resonant. It specifically captures the unwillingness to let go before securing the next grip.

- **Q:** Can this word accidentally offend someone?
  **A:** Accusing someone of monkey-barring is a serious allegation about their character and relationship integrity. It's essentially calling them emotionally dishonest and fearful of being alone. Use with caution.

- **Q:** Is this a passing trend or here to stay?
  **A:** GEBILAOWANG predicts "monkey-barring" will persist as vocabulary. The behavior it describes has existed forever; the term just finally gives it a name. As long as dating apps make it easy to line up alternatives, monkey-barring will continue.

- **Q:** How do I explain this quickly to someone who's out of the loop?
  **A:** "It's when someone won't break up with their current partner until they've already found a new one — like swinging from monkey bars without letting go. It's a toxic dating behavior that went viral on TikTok."
