Boy math is girl math's revenge arc. Where girl math playfully justified questionable spending (cash isn't real money, returns are free money), boy math aims at male double standards and personal accounting of a different kind.

The textbook example: boy math is being 5'11" but saying 6'0". Actually, scratch that. The real textbook example is boy math being 5'9" and saying 6'0".

## The clapback origin

The term showed up in late 2023, weeks after girl math went mega-viral. As The Cut framed it in a September 28, 2023 piece, if girl math was women joking about their own financial logic, boy math was women pointing out the logic men use on everything else. The height inflation gag became the flagship example almost immediately, followed by a whole catalog: watching three YouTube videos counts as "doing research," knowing a guy who knows a guy counts as a connection, and going to the gym twice a month counts as being "really into fitness."

The meme even escaped the internet: U.S. Representatives Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez and Ilhan Omar both publicly used "boy math" to dunk on opponents. When a slang term reaches Congress, it's not niche anymore.

## Classic boy math

The genre has greatest hits. Height rounding is the eternal number one. Close behind: the age math of dating ("she's mature for her age" from a guy who is neither mature nor young), and the financial math of calling women gold diggers while splitting the check on a first date. That last one is the community favorite, the so-called gold digger paradox: being terrified of gold diggers while having no gold.

Honest assessment? It's less clever than girl math. Joking on yourself takes charm. Joking on other people is easier and cheaper. But as a clapback it does its job, and the height jokes are timeless. Sometimes a meme just picks a lane and stays in it.

## Boy math vs girl math

If you're fuzzy on the difference: [girl math](/slang/girl-math/) = self-directed, playful, about money. Boy math = other-directed, pointed, about double standards. Same naming format, opposite intent. One says "we're all a little delusional, haha." The other says "you, specifically."

You'll still see new entries posted under videos where a guy explains something with total confidence and zero information. Comment sections do the rest: "the boy math is mathing."

> "him: i don't need directions, i have a good sense of direction. also him: lost in the parking garage for 20 minutes. boy math"

> @noturfavmaddy: he said he's "basically 6 foot" at 5'10 😭 the boy math is strong with this one
